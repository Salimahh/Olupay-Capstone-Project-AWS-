We chose RDS MySQL over Aurora for OluPay's KYC database because Aurora's cost (~$0.10/hour minimum) exceeds our $200/month steady-state budget when combined with other services, while RDS MySQL on db.t3.micro fits within Free Tier for development and early production. Aurora's superior throughput (up to 5x MySQL) and automatic failover would be justified for the payments ledger at 2 million users peak, but for the KYC store — which handles read-heavy, lower-frequency identity verification rather than high-frequency salary-day transactions — RDS Multi-AZ with Read Replica provides sufficient resilience at a fraction of the cost. If OluPay scales beyond 5 million users, migrating the payments ledger specifically to Aurora Serverless v2 would be the logical next step.
